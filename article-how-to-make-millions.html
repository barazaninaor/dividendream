The Path to Millions

“Do you know the only thing that gives me pleasure? It’s to see my dividends coming in.” — John D. Rockefeller
When we invest in stocks, we’re acquiring a tiny ownership stake in a publicly traded entity. The primary objective of any successful business is to expand and enhance its profits. After a period of rapid growth, established companies like Coca-Cola reach a pivotal juncture: they become massive cash-flow machines that return profits directly to you.

Why Companies Pay You

The term “dividend” originates from the Latin word “dividendum,” meaning “to divide.” Successful companies distribute these profits for three primary reasons:

  • Rewarding Investors: Acknowledging your role as a stakeholder in their success.
  • Market Appeal: Dividends make stable companies highly attractive to long-term capital.
  • Proving Resilience: Consistent payments signal elite quality and financial reliability.

Intriguing Insights

To make millions, you must understand the mechanics of the game. Here is what smart investors know:

  • Fixed Income: If a company pays a $5 dividend, you get $5 regardless of the stock price.
  • Payment Frequency: Most US companies pay 4 times a year, though some offer monthly paychecks.
  • Taxation: Dividends are subject to capital gains tax; planning is key to maximizing net wealth.
